In the top pane on the left-hand side you will find a black window. In that window there is text that has been marked-up with HTML. I use the term marked-up, because HTML stands for Hyper Text Markup Language.

Simple emphasis and boldening

You can seperate paragraphs of text using <p> "tags" or "elements". You can emphasize text using <em> tags, and you can bolden text using <strong> tags. Although it can get obnoxious pretty quick. Be glad you didn't have to deal with <blink> tags!

These HTML elements, or HTML "tags" need a beginning and an end. So if the start tag is <p>, the end tag needs to be </p>. Notice the forward-slash, that is the key to closing an element.

So far we have just learned a few ways of "decorating" text. Usually that is the job of CSS but let's ignore the for just the moment, and continue on with HTML.

People love making lists

In HTML you can define lists of things, for instance, this is called an unordered list:

Fruits
  • Apples
  • Oranges
  • Pears
  • Blueberries

But not all lists are unordered, some are ordered, like this one right here:

  1. Put on your VR headseat
  2. Press the big play button in front of you
  3. TAKE COVER!
  4. Keep moving, camper

Lists are also used in interesting ways like horizontal menus, usually seen at the top of web pages. They are also great candidates for Chapter lists and tables of contents.

Using HTML as structure

There are two kinds of "tags", the kind that have what we call a "block" display, meaning it takes up a block of space, and "inline", and they just flow.

Let's look at some of these mysterious block elements, starting with the <div> tag. This tag will break the flow. An example:

I am a div, and therefore, block
I am a strong, and therefore, inline I am an em, and therefore, inline. Notice I did not "break the flow"
Here comes another <div> to break the flow! Jerk thinks he is a Bizkit.

Typically, a <div> tag is used to block off some text, like a section, or a row, or a "box" of some sorts.

Without the help of CSS divs don't really do much, come to think of it!
